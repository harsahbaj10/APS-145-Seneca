Animal-Mart Pet Supplies (Sale/Promotion Week)

System Modification Details

Promotions

  1. For each item over $50, a sample-sized bag of animal food will be given to the customer (customer will decide what food: Cat, Dog, Rodent, Reptile, Fish). In cases where the customer earns more than one free sample, the customer may choose a variety of food samples to until fulfillment is reached.

Note: Inventory must be maintained, so for each free sample given out to a customer, a reduction in inventory for that type of sample food must be deducted.

  1. Customers will be rewarded based on the number of items purchased with a coupon that can be used on their next purchase:
  2. 1-3 : Receives no coupon but is given a thank-you with a smile
  3. 4-8 : Receive a 5%-off coupon for use on their next purchase; thank the customer and give a smile
  4. 9-12 : Receive a 10%-off coupon for use on their next purchase; thank the customer and give a smile
  5. 13+ : Receive a 25%-off coupon for use on their next purchase; thank the customer and give a smile

Note: If a coupon is awarded, it will be generated on the receipt as a barcode (with corresponding text so the customer knows its value). The receipt will be used as the coupon in their next purchase. The company also requires the system to keep track of how many coupons have been generated along with their respective worth/value.

Your Task

Build upon the existing cashier point of sales system (POS) to process these types of sale/promotion transactions. This logic will build upon the existing POS process. You can assume there are the following existing sub-processes that do not need to be defined and you may call from your solution:

Scan Item: Process will deal with the scanning of an item and fetching the data from the system returning the necessary

details about the item; No data will be returned if there is an error.

Add Item to Transactions: Process will adds the item details to the POS transactions session and deals with updating the

system inventory.

Note: Requires a list of the current transactions and the details of the new item to be added

to the transactions as input to this process

Generate Receipt: Process will deal with getting customer payment and generates the itemized receipt

Note: Requires a list of transactions as input

(Transactions is a list of product information [items] that are being purchased.)

Pseudo Code

Data Structures

ItemInfo: UPC, Price

CouponInfo: UPC, DiscountAmount

Variables

itemCount (total # of items being purchased)

ItemInfo::Item (single item)

ItemInfo::Sample (represents a single free sample product)

ItemInfo::Transactions (is a list of items for the purchase session)

CouponInfo::Coupon (represents a single coupon offering)
